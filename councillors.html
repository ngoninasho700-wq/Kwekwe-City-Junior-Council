How the Junior Council Works

The Kwekwe City Junior Council is made up of elected Junior Councillors representing schools and wards across the City of Kwekwe. Working together with the Executive Council, members discuss issues affecting young people, develop solutions and organise programmes that improve their schools and communities.

Junior Councillors participate in leadership training, environmental projects, educational campaigns, child rights advocacy, community service and partnerships with Kwekwe City Council. Through these activities they gain valuable leadership experience while making meaningful contributions to the development of their city.

Key Responsibilities

Youth Representation

Representing the views, ideas and concerns of children and young people across Kwekwe.

Community Service

Organising volunteer programmes, clean-up campaigns and community development initiatives.

Environmental Action

Promoting environmental sustainability through awareness campaigns and conservation projects.
